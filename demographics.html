Analysis of Big 5 Personality Test






Feature Varience Over The Clusters

Several demographic features were considered with respect to how their distributions might vary over the five data clusters. The demographic features tested included gender, age, race, and left/right handedness. For each of these features, hypothesis testing was completed.

While the specific ANOVA numbers varied, the overall results on all demographic features were like those demonstrated by the Age boxplot above. Means and data dispersions were very similar across the clusters. ANOVA tests all revealed high p-values, significantly above 0.05. In all cases of demographic data, we failed to reject the null hypothesis that clusters were similarly distirbuted among the relevant populations.

Hypothesis Testing

  • Gender: 0.4265
  • Age: 0.5695
  • Race: 0.4551
  • Handedness: 0.8618

Null Hypothesis (H0): Clusters are similar across features such as gender, age, handedness, or race.

Conclusion: We fail to reject H0, because p values are all above 0.05.
